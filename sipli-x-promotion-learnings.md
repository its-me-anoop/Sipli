# Sipli — X (Twitter) Promotion Learnings

A living reference for crafting Sipli posts on X. Always consult before writing a new tweet. Append new learnings at the bottom after every campaign.

---

## Competitive scan (April 2026)

Analysed 10 recent promotional posts from hydration / water-tracker / wellness apps on X — including Dewdrop (@theWatcher_9984), Waterllama, Terekhin Ivan's Water Tracker (@TerekhinIvan), Daily Water Reminder (via Product Hunt), Natia Kurdadze's hydration tracker listing, plus 4 adjacent build-in-public / wellness-app posts.

### What works

1. **Character / mascot storytelling.** Dewdrop's "Dewey the water droplet has more emotional range than most apps" hook got 38 engagements on a tiny account. Giving the product a face > listing features.
2. **Specific numbers, not adjectives.** "4 months of solo development", "82 organic installs", "₹90L/month" — concrete numbers beat "amazing" every time.
3. **Build-in-public framing.** Founders sharing behind-the-scenes (revenue, installs, churn, the one feature that finally clicked) outperform polished launch posts.
4. **One idea per tweet.** Posts that try to list 5 features tank. Posts with one sharp observation + one CTA win.
5. **Short hook, expand below the fold.** "Let me tell you about Dewey" → then the breakdown. The first line is the whole game.
6. **Visual consistency.** Dark-mode app screenshots inside a device frame, on a solid coloured background, consistently outperform raw screenshot dumps.
7. **Replies / quote-tweets as distribution.** Founders replying to their own thread with new angles get more reach than standalone posts.

### What doesn't work

- Generic marketing copy ("Stay hydrated with our smart water tracker!"). Zero engagement.
- Hashtag stuffing (5+ hashtags). Feels spammy, kills organic reach.
- Emoji walls. One, maybe two tasteful emoji max.
- "Download now!" CTAs. Too salesy — link in the tweet body performs better when framed as "if you want to try it" or "it's on the App Store".
- Stock-photo hero images of someone smiling with a glass of water. Everyone skips them.
- Launch-day "We're live on Product Hunt!!!" posts with no story around them.

### Hook patterns that stop the scroll

- **The confession** — "I drank 4 cups of coffee before noon and zero water. Again."
- **The realisation** — "Turns out my 'daily water goal' changes by 800ml when it's 32°C vs 18°C outside."
- **The character** — "Meet Dewey" / "Meet [feature name]"
- **The unpopular opinion** — "Your hydration goal shouldn't be a fixed number."
- **The build-in-public reveal** — "I built this because I kept forgetting [thing]." / "Month 2 of Sipli: here's what I learned."
- **The specific moment** — "3pm headache. Checked Sipli. 600ml behind. Every. Single. Time."

### Tone calibration for Sipli (founder voice)

- Write like a DM to a friend, not a press release.
- Lowercase is fine. Em-dashes are fine. Contractions are fine.
- Be honest about trade-offs ("it's not going to fix your life, but it'll make you notice how little water you actually drink").
- Never say "game-changer", "revolutionary", "seamless", "leverage", "unlock", "hydration journey", "elevate your wellness".
- It's okay to be a little self-deprecating.
- Always mention a single feature — never a list.

### CTA patterns that work

- "It's on the App Store if you want to try it: [link]"
- "Free on iOS: [link]"
- "Built it solo — would mean a lot if you gave it a look: [link]"
- Avoid: "Download now!", "Get yours today!", "Don't miss out!"

### Hashtag guidance

- 1 hashtag is the sweet spot. 2 max.
- Best performers for this niche: `#buildinpublic`, `#indiedev`, `#iOSDev`, `#SwiftUI`, `#Hydration`
- Avoid stuffing wellness tags (`#WellnessJourney #HealthIsWealth #HydrationNation`). They read as spam.

### Visual style that works (1200x675)

- **Dark background** — deep navy / near-black / pure black.
- **One subtle accent colour** — a single brand hue (Sipli blue/cyan works well).
- **Device in context** — phone at an angle, partial crop, or floating. Not a dead-centre PNG dump.
- **Minimal text** — max ~6 words on the image, big and bold. The tweet copy does the heavy lifting.
- **Atmosphere over content** — gradient glow, soft shadow, single-source lighting. Feels premium, not cluttered.
- **Negative space** — let the image breathe. Don't fill every pixel.
- **No screenshot grids.** One hero view of the app, tightly framed.
