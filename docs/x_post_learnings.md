## Patterns that work

**Hook in the first line.** Every strong post earns the click with one punchy sentence: a pain ("got fed up paying $15/week"), a feeling ("drinking water should feel less like a chore"), a claim ("solo, with zero budget"), or a fresh metaphor ("turns your water into a living plant"). Avoid starting with "Check out" or "I made".

**One-sentence positioning.** Say what the app is and who it's for in <=15 words. "X is Y for Z who want A." Sipli version: "Sipli is a playful hydration tracker for people who want water to feel like a game, not a chore."

**Concrete features as tight bullets (3–5 max).** Bullets beat paragraphs for scroll-stopping readability. Use 🚀💧🏋️ emojis sparingly — one per bullet, not two. Avoid generic ("great UI") — lean on specifics ("adapts to weather and workouts", "18 beverage types", "data stays on-device").

**Differentiator sentence.** One line about what makes Sipli different: privacy-first (no cloud, no account), gamified (quests/XP/streaks), or smart (weather + workouts). Don't try to say all three in one post — pick one per post.

**Clear CTA + App Store link.** Every post ends with the link. Prefer a line like "Try it on iOS → https://apps.apple.com/us/app/sipli/id6758851574" over a raw URL. Place it on its own line.

**Ask for something small.** Top-performing launch posts ask explicitly: "a 5★ rating means the world", "roast the features", "reply with your hydration goal". Always make the ask low-cost.

**One strong visual.** Posts with a single striking image or 2×2 screenshot grid outperform text-only. Sipli has `sipli_promo.png` (1600×900 branded card) and `appstore-screenshots/` — rotate these so the feed isn't repetitive.

**Hashtags are background, not foreground.** 2–4 max, at the end. Default stack: `#iOS #SwiftUI #IndieDev #BuildInPublic`. Wellness angle: add `#Hydration` or `#HealthKit`. Never hashtag every word.

**Use build-in-public narrative once per launch cycle.** "First-ever iOS app", "solo + zero budget", "built what I kept wishing existed" — these humanize and drive replies. Don't overuse; rotate with feature-focused posts.

**Mention the tech if relevant.** SwiftUI / HealthKit / WeatherKit mentions attract the iOS-dev audience and earn boosts from that community.

## Patterns to avoid

- Burying the App Store link inside a thread continuation.
- Multi-paragraph walls of text with no visual.
- >5 hashtags or hashtags mid-sentence.
- Vague claims ("best hydration app ever", "life-changing") without specifics.
- Reusing the exact same copy more than once — always vary hook + angle.
- Emoji spam (>1 per line, or >5 in a post).
- Starting with "Hey everyone" / "Excited to share" — weak openers.
- Fake urgency ("only today!") unless there's a real coupon/sale.
- Never include download links that aren't the canonical `apps.apple.com/us/app/sipli/id6758851574`.

## Sipli-specific rules

- **Always include the App Store link: `https://apps.apple.com/us/app/sipli/id6758851574`** on its own line, even for reply posts.
- **Never claim "plastic bottles saved"** or any numeric sustainability stat — Sipli does not measure that. Use "refill, don't rebuy" framing instead.
- **Privacy angle is a superpower.** "No accounts, no cloud, no tracking. Data stays on your device." — use it on at least 1 in 3 posts.
- **Rotate the hook across these angles** so followers see variety:
  1. Playful / gamified ("hydration, made a game")
  2. Smart / adaptive (weather + workouts + HealthKit)
  3. Privacy-first (on-device only)
  4. Dev / build-in-public (SwiftUI, HealthKit, WeatherKit)
  5. Habit / wellness benefit (headaches, energy, focus)
  6. Earth-week / refill (only during Earth Week 2026, Apr 20–26)
- **Default hashtag stack:** `#iOS #SwiftUI #Hydration #BuildInPublic` (pick 2–3).
- **Always attach an image** — `/Users/anoopjose/Projects/WaterQuest/sipli_promo.png` is the default launch card.
- **Character budget:** aim for 220–260 characters of copy so the image and link render cleanly; allow up to ~500 if the structure warrants bullets.
- **Tone:** warm, slightly playful, human. Never corporate. First-person ("I built Sipli…") is fine when build-in-public angle is used.
